# Map 2 — South Trail · "The Long Light"  *(first region)*

> A sunlit switchback down into the canyon, a pop idol's missing centerpiece, and
> a self-proclaimed Duchess of the Abyss who has filed a *domain claim.* Vesper's
> first real day on the job, and everyone on it is ridiculous.

- **Act:** 1 · **The one feeling:** *cozy adventure tipping into first spectacle*
  — warm light, a horde to carve, a clown to bonk.
- **Movement identity:** **horizontal flow** — flash-jump chains across measured
  gaps, one-way platform descents, hop/flutter to fine-tune an arc. The first map
  that's *about* covering ground stylishly.
- **New stakes:** the first **real hordes** (slice-through spectacle, GAME.md §5)
  and the first **boss** (Gemma, comedic). Stardust starts flowing (§8).

## Story / quest beats

- **Q2 "The Show Must Go On"** *(side, early)* — Lumi radios: her stage's
  centerpiece, the **Big Gem** (a vein-charged prop that runs her finale
  lighting), bounced off the stage during the leak spike and a **Fold Slime ate
  it** and went down the trail. Worse — the slime **split**, so the Gem's charge
  scattered across **three** slimes. Get them before her eight-o'clock set.
- **Q3 "Domain of the Dread Duchess"** *(main, boss)* — at the trail's overlook,
  **Gemma** has set up a "throne" (a folding camp chair she has hard-light-
  rendered to look like a throne) and declared the canyon her eternal domain.
  She's here to out-survey Vesper and prove the Works should've sent *her.* Duel.

## Layout — zone walkthrough

A descending sunlit trail, the canyon opening wider on your right as you drop —
the vista promising the Gulch below (teach by terrain). Linear with horde pockets
and one optional vertical detour.

- **The Trailhead** (entry from the Rim portal). A breezy shelf; the town's noise
  fades behind you. First **Fold Slimes** plop down the path ahead — they hop, and
  **split when sliced**, so the trail teaches "a horde *multiplies* if you're
  sloppy; carve clean lines."
- **The Switchbacks.** Stacked one-way ledges zig-zagging down — the descent gym.
  **Pebble Drones** roll along the straights (charge in a line; bait them off
  ledges). Stardust rewards the player who flash-jumps the corners instead of
  walking them.
- **The Sunshelf.** A wide flat ledge — the **horde arena.** Slimes + drones spawn
  in a satisfying excess; **Cactus Satellites** dot the floor as orbiting-spike
  hazards you fight *around.* This is the power-fantasy intro: carve a line, build
  momentum, pop a crowd. (Combat juice — hitstop/shake/combo — is M12.)
- **The Cracked Span.** A broken stretch crossed by **flash-jump gaps** (the
  movement test), with a **hidden portal** behind a teleport-through thin wall
  (secret nook → a stardust vault; rewards mode-B teleport curiosity).
- **The Overlook.** The trail's scenic end and **Gemma's "domain"** — a broad
  arena ledge over the gulch drop, ringed by her hard-light "dread banners." The
  boss space.

## Mobs

- **Fold Slime** — the Q2 vehicle: hops, **splits on hit** (slice clean or drown
  in slimes). **Pebble Drone** — straight-line charger; environmental kills.
  **Cactus Satellite** — stationary orbiting-spike hazard; arena texture.
  **Star Mote** — ambient drift. **Thread Imps** appear *only* in the Gemma fight
  (her "minions").

## Set-piece — the Gemma duel

The first **boss**, played for **comedy**, not difficulty (GAME.md §5: bosses are
rare real fights — but Gemma is the *recurring clown*, the exception that proves
it). Design within reach of the moveset; full boss-system is M12, so this is the
target:

- **Phase 1 — The Monologue.** Gemma summons "her" Thread Imps (they were just
  passing through) and hurls slow, telegraphed hard-light "abyssal" projectiles
  while monologuing. You dodge with the moveset and bonk the imps; her shield
  drops when she pauses for dramatic effect (she always does).
- **Phase 2 — The Tantrum.** Down a third, she drops the sing-song and *tries*
  — faster, sloppier, genuinely a little dangerous, very whiny. Teleport-phase
  (mode B) through her big "ultimate" beam; punish the recovery.
- **Defeat.** She does not get unmade — she gets *embarrassed.* Wings fritzing,
  she flops off the overlook into the gulch updraft, vowing a comeback in a
  better-lit venue. Drops a half-melted "dread banner" (a cosmetic / lore gag).
  The gulch portal lights in her wake.

## Scope & reuse notes

- **Reuses:** tilemap + one-way platforms, the moveset (flash-jump/teleport/hop
  are the stars here), the portal prop from Map 1, stardust drops.
- **New (small):** Fold Slime (split logic), Pebble Drone (charge), Cactus
  Satellite (orbit hazard), Thread Imp (flit) — all cheap cute sprites + simple
  behaviors. The **carry-the-Gem** object (a held prop → previews M-physics grab).
- **Leans on later:** real horde combat + boss feel is **M12**; Gemma's "boss"
  here is a design target greyboxable with telegraphed projectiles + the slice
  stub. Until M12, this map is playable as a pure **traversal** route (Q2/Q3 light
  up when combat lands).
- **Cut lines:** if the split-slime puzzle frustrates, the Gem can ride **one**
  slow slime (chase, not herd). Gemma's phase 2 can drop entirely — the monologue
  + a single bonk is the whole joke if the fight fights us.
