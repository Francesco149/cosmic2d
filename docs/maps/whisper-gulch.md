# Map 3 — Whisper Gulch · "The Whisper Below"  *(vertical gym + first dread)*

> A crack in the world barely wider than her hat. The festival noise can't reach
> down here. For the first time since she fell in, Vesper is alone with the thing
> she actually came to measure — and it's bigger than the form has a box for.

- **Act:** 1 (the turn) · **The one feeling:** *intimacy curdling into unease* —
  cozy gives way to the first real **cosmic dread** (GAME.md §1, used with a light
  hand). The town was warm; the deep seam is *eerie-beautiful and wrong.*
- **Movement identity:** **verticality** — grapple chains, up-jumps, measured
  drops, and teleport-phasing past thin walls. The grapple/up-jump gym (the
  moveset's "down is a direction you platform, not fall").
- **New stakes:** the first **armored** mob (Glyph Beetle), the first **work-order
  from the Works**, and the first time the music drops out.

## Story / quest beats — Q4 "The Whisper Below"

1. **Down after her.** Gemma flopped into the gulch updraft; the trail down
   follows. The leak gets *denser* as you descend — more creatures, brighter
   veins, the air humming.
2. **The glyphs that watch.** The walls are covered in **petroglyphs** (Sunwright
   echoes — 3D-canon atmosphere). As Vesper's glow passes, a few seem to **track
   her.** Nobody explains it. (She files it under "ambient phenomena." She does not
   like it.)
3. **The grapple dark.** The floor gives out to a void; the route is grapple-node
   to grapple-node over the deep (the gym crescendo). **Glyph Beetles** crawl the
   walls — armored, shells must be cracked — guarding the descent.
4. **The Deep Vein.** The bottom: a vein the size of a cathedral, leaking so hard
   the rock around it has gone half-unreal — colors bleeding, geometry stuttering
   (a cheap, gorgeous foreshadow of the **Sweep**, run in reverse: reality leaking
   *out* instead of being cleaned *away*). It's the most beautiful and most wrong
   thing she's seen. **The cozy music drops to a single held tone.**
5. **The work-order.** Her comms chime — cold, clipped, from the **Works**:
   *survey overdue · generator flagged UNSTABLE · escalation pending.* The word
   under the word is **Sweep.** She looks at the vein, then up the gulch toward the
   town's faint glow. The tsundere mask slips — one rare sincere beat, alone where
   no one can see — and she starts stabilizing the vein anyway, telling no one.
6. **Up and out.** A grapple/up-jump ascent back to the light; the Rim restages to
   **night** when she returns (her glow lights the empty plaza). She does not file
   the form. (Act 1 button; the dread is planted, the cozy is now *stakes.*)

## Layout — zone walkthrough

A linear vertical descent with pocket chambers — a slot canyon is corridor-shaped
*honestly*, the cheapest "real place" we'll build: two sculpted walls, a sky slit,
light shafts. Verticality does the variety (3D `slot-canyon.md` lesson).

- **The Throat.** The entry crack: descending drops + one **teleport-phase**
  through a thin sealed wall (mode B). Every footstep echoes — audio carries this
  map more than geometry (whisper ambience).
- **The Cathedral.** A tall sculpted bell of striped stone, one diagonal light
  shaft full of drifting motes — the postcard. Energy veins surface here, thin and
  glowing, brightening as she nears (the glow-reactive intro, unexplained).
- **The Glyphworks.** Petroglyph walls (beat 2) + the first **Glyph Beetles**
  (crack the shell, then file them). A hidden alcove behind a **flutter-reach**
  ledge holds a lore pickup.
- **The Stitch-Dark.** The grapple gym: the floor is gone, the route is node-to-
  node over the void, nodes pulsing on a loose beat. Miss = a soft drop to a ledge
  + a short walk back (no death). The crescendo of the moveset.
- **The Deep Vein.** The bottom chamber (beats 4–5): the cathedral-sized leak, the
  half-unreal rock, the held tone, the work-order. The dread set-piece.

## Mobs

- **Glyph Beetle** — armored crawler; **crack the shell** (a stagger window), then
  file it. The first mob that asks for *timing*, not just carving. **Fold Slime**
  + **Star Mote** thin the descent. Optional: a single **Mirror Wisp** tease in
  the Deep Vein (blinks away when approached — a "what was that" hook for later).

## Set-piece — the Deep Vein

The game's **first quiet, strange beat.** Everything that's been warm and busy —
the music, the chatter, Gemma's noise — *stops.* One held tone. A leak so large
the world around it forgets the rules. Vesper, alone, does the small filing
gesture she's done a hundred times today — and for one frame it reads exactly like
the **Sweep** the player has been dreading. Then the work-order chimes, and she
chooses the town over the form without a word. Costs us: one ambient-audio
duck, one "unreal-rock" shader pass (the Sweep's blockout palette, run *backwards*
— the M-look LUT seed), and a single line of UI text.

## Scope & reuse notes

- **Reuses:** tilemap verticality + one-way platforms, the grapple/up-jump/
  teleport moveset (M7 — this map is *built* for them), the night restage from
  Map 1, the glow-as-light-source (her sprite already tints).
- **New (small):** Glyph Beetle (armor/stagger), grapple-node prop + pulse
  sequencing, the petroglyph "wake" (a flat shader-anim cutout — cheap), the
  "unreal-rock" shader/LUT pass (doubles as the M-look + Sweep-palette seed), the
  work-order comms UI (text-only).
- **Leans on later:** the dread shader/LUT is **M-look**; the lore-collectible
  system + farmables are **M13**; armored-mob combat feel is **M12**. The map is
  fully playable today as a **grapple/teleport traversal gym** — the dread beat is
  a cutscene-light overlay we layer on once the shader exists.
- **Cut lines:** the watching-glyphs never threaten — atmosphere only. If the
  grapple-void crossing fights the feel, the nodes get more generous before the
  void gets deeper. The Mirror Wisp tease is the first thing cut if the Deep Vein
  reads busy — the *silence* is the content there.
