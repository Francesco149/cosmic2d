# Rim Hub — scene script · "First Survey" (Q1)

The fleshed-out dialogue + staging for Map 1 ([design page](rim-hub.md)). This is
the screenplay layer: **who's in each scene, where it plays (by greybox marker),
what it looks/sounds like, and how the lines land.** Triggers/logic come later —
this is the content those triggers will fire.

**How to read.** Everyone speaks in **voice-blips** (Banjo/Undertale style), not
full VO; the text is the subtitle. *(Italics in parens = performance/staging
direction.)* Tone is **cozy and comedic** (STORY §2) — Vesper is a **tsundere**
(prideful, flustered, secretly soft); play every beat for warmth or a laugh.
**Optional barks** are short, skippable, fire on proximity/idle. Markers
referenced (e.g. `FOLD-IN`, `LUMI'S STAGE`) are the labeled spots in the greybox.

**Cast on this map:** **Vesper** (you), **Lumi** (idol), the **Shopkeep** (a
laconic ranger — Bridger-cameo TBD), **Townsfolk** (ambient, faceless-cute),
**Star Motes** (no voice; the gentle leak-critters). *Gemma is NOT here yet —
she debuts on the South Trail.*

---

## S0 — Cold open: "The Drop"  *(cutscene-light · marker: `SKY-SEAM` → `FOLD-IN`)*

**Staging.** Black, then a field of stars. A pair of pristine gloved hands
unpins a small bright object — *a generator, folded down to pocket size* — and
turns it to read a softly glowing readout. Camera pushes into the object: inside
is a warm canyon at golden hour. She leans closer. Closer. The readout blips an
alarm. She leans *too* close — and **falls in.** The fall is the title card and
the air-control tutorial; the **`SKY-SEAM`** (the fold-door she fell through)
stays parked as a bright stitch in the sky for the rest of the game.

> **VESPER:** *(reading, clipped/bored)* Generator C-3. Output nominal. Casing
> integrity… nominal. Lifesign exhaust… *(pause)* …higher than filed.
> **VESPER:** *(leaning in)* That's not in the spec. Why is there so much—
> *(the readout shrieks; she overbalances)* — oh n—

**Land** hat-first on the **`FOLD-IN`** ledge above the plaza. She sits up,
catches her hat, and is immediately, furiously composed.

> **VESPER:** *(to absolutely no one, brushing off)* …That was a controlled
> descent. *(beat)* I meant to do that. I'm an **Auditor.**

*Purpose:* establish the architect/Works premise in 15 seconds, teach the jump,
plant the sky-seam, and nail Vesper's voice (pride first, fluster underneath).

## S1 — "Reception"  *(plaza · markers: `RIM GATE / PLAZA`, `PETROGLYPH WALL`)*

**Staging.** Vesper drops into a **festival**: bunting, lanterns, distant music,
**Townsfolk** milling. It is aggressively cozy and she hates that she doesn't
hate it. A handful of **Star Motes** drift through the plaza, bonking lanterns —
the game's first "enemies," used as the bonk/slice tutorial-by-terrain (no
threat; they pop into stardust). A townsperson mistakes her for festival talent.

> **TOWNSPERSON:** Ooh, are you the new act? Love the armor. Very *spooky-space.*
> **VESPER:** It's not a— this is **standard-issue.**
> **TOWNSPERSON:** Standard-issue spooky-space! *(wanders off delighted)*
> **VESPER:** *(flat)* …I'm going to file this whole town.

**Star Mote bonk** (first one you hit):

> **VESPER:** *(a Mote drifts into her, she swats it; it pops to stardust)* —
> A loose pixel of starlight. The seam's *leaking.* *(she watches the stardust)*
> …Huh. It files clean. *(catches herself enjoying it)* Don't.

**Optional barks** (idle / examining):
- *(at the `PETROGLYPH WALL`)* **VESPER:** Someone carved the wall. On purpose.
  With *spirals.* …These people decorate their **rocks.** *(a beat of genuine
  wonder, instantly suppressed)* …Noted. Moving on.
- *(idle in plaza)* **VESPER:** I'll just stabilize the leak, file the survey,
  and go. In and out. A normal amount of caring, which is **none.**

*Purpose:* the world reacts before any quest UI; teach bonk/stardust; first
"creatures = leaking reality" line; first tiny crack in the ice (the rocks).

---

## S2 — "Stage Left"  *(meet Lumi · marker: `LUMI'S STAGE`)*

**Staging.** **Lumi** is rehearsing on the **`LUMI'S STAGE`** platform — full
idol sparkle, a tiny crowd of Townsfolk, the **vein footlights** flickering
because the leak keeps browning them out. Mid-twirl she clocks Vesper, gasps with
delight, and drops the stage voice the second the song ends.

> **LUMI:** *(on stage, radiant)* — and that's why you NEVER give up, Riiim! ☆
> *(spots Vesper; gasp)* **Ohmygosh.** A rival act. The horns girl SAID she was
> bringing competition—
> **VESPER:** What "horns girl."
> **LUMI:** *(stage voice off, suddenly dry)* Tall. Purple. Monologues. Filed a
> "domain claim" on the canyon last week, whatever that is. *(beat)* You two have
> the same *energy,* actually.
> **VESPER:** *(bristling)* I have **nothing** in common with— *(the footlights
> gutter; Lumi flinches)*
> **LUMI:** *(quiet, real)* …The lights run off the veins. They keep dying.
> *(stage voice snapping back on)* But the show goes ON! Probably! Hey — if
> you're so official, maybe go *officially* tell the leak to knock it off?
> **VESPER:** That's… *(almost) not how surveys work. (it is exactly how it works)*
> …I'll look into it. For the **record.** Not for the show.
> **LUMI:** *(grinning)* Sure. For the record. *(waves)* Break a leg, record girl!

*Purpose:* introduce Lumi (warm/dry duality), plant Gemma offscreen ("horns
girl"), set up the leak as the town's real problem, and start the
Vesper↔Lumi "two performers who can't drop the act" rapport. Seeds Q2.

---

## S3 — "Bitter and Warm"  *(the shop · marker: `COFFEE SHOP`)*

**Staging.** The cliffside **`COFFEE SHOP`**: the **Shopkeep** (laconic ranger,
Bridger-cameo TBD), a noticeboard (= the diegetic menu/help), steam, the smell of
coffee implied by everyone's contentment. He does not look up. The whole scene is
two unbothered people and one increasingly destabilized auditor.

> **SHOPKEEP:** Coffee.
> **VESPER:** I don't require sustenance. I'm a self-contained—
> **SHOPKEEP:** *(slides her a cup)* It's warm. It's bitter. You share it. 's
> about the only thing we got figured out up here.
> **VESPER:** *(holding the cup like evidence)* …This is a *bribe.*
> **SHOPKEEP:** It's a coffee.
> **VESPER:** *(does not drink it. does not put it down. holds it the entire rest
> of the level.)*

**Noticeboard** (examine → opens the help/menu, diegetic):

> **VESPER:** *(reading)* "Lost & found. Idol auditions. BEWARE the ravens."
> *(beat)* …A community board. For a *byproduct.* *(softer than she means)*
> …That's almost organized.

*Purpose:* the shop/menu hub; the coffee running gag (the cup she won't admit she
likes — a tiny tsundere visual throughline); a hint the town is more "someone"
than "exhaust." Bridger-cameo flavor without committing.

---

## S4 — "Adequate Lighting"  *(light the trail · markers: `PORTAL → South Trail`, then the dusk restage)*

**Staging.** The leak is thickest at the rim's far arm, where the
**`PORTAL → South Trail`** sign sits dark. Reaching it across the **`ROOFTOP
GYM`** (the movement payoff) and stabilizing the local vein **lights the portal**
— and the light **cascades**: the trailhead, then the plaza lanterns, then
Lumi's footlights, catching one by one as the town tips into **dusk.** Lumi's
evening set kicks in. It is the coziest the game has been; Vesper is furious
about how much she likes it.

> **VESPER:** *(stabilizing the vein, all business)* Sealing the leak point.
> Output returning to tolerance. There. *(the portal lights; the cascade begins)*
> **VESPER:** *(watching the town glow, the cup still in her hand)* …
> **LUMI:** *(distant, on stage, delighted)* The lights! They're back! RIIIM, give
> it UP for— *(squints)* — for the spooky-space record girl!! ☆
> **VESPER:** *(to herself, very quietly)* …Tch. *(beat)* It's **adequate
> lighting.** That's all. *(beat)* …Don't read into it. *(she finally sips the
> coffee. just once. doesn't comment.)*

**Button bark** (as you step into the lit portal):
> **VESPER:** Survey continues south. *(beat)* …I'll be back before the set ends.
> Not that it matters.

*Purpose:* the "I did a thing" payoff = the portal + the **day→dusk restage**
(STORY/GAME's cheapest emotional lever); the warm button on Q1; the coffee
finally sipped = the ice cracked one degree. Hands off to the South Trail.

## Ambient & idle (sprinkle throughout)

- **Townsfolk barks** (faceless-cute, random): *"Festival's better with the lights
  on, huh?"* · *"You see the purple lady? Real dramatic."* · *"My cousin swears
  the rocks move at night."* (a free dread hint for the acute — Sunwright echoes.)
- **Vesper idle** (if you stand still): *"…I could file from here. I'm not going
  to. But I could."* · *"Six hundred shifts of training and I'm holding a
  beverage."*
- **Sandbox nook** (`SANDBOX NOOK`): knocking a crate around → **VESPER:** *"Matter
  responds to the field. Obviously. …Do it again."* (the gmod-sandbox seed, GAME §7.)

## Notes for when we wire it (later)

- Q1 critical path is **reach the portal**; every scene above is optional-skippable
  except S0 (cold open) and the portal-light beat (S4). Keep barks interruptible.
- The **coffee cup** is a tiny persistent prop on Vesper from S3 → end of S4 (art:
  a held-cup overlay or a frame variant). Worth it — it's the whole tsundere gag.
- Lumi's "horns girl" line is the **only** Gemma setup needed before the Trail.
- Dread here is **one** quiet hint (the moving-rocks bark) — keep it that light.
