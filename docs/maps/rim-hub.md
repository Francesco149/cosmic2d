# Map 1 — Rim Hub · "First Survey"  *(hub + tutorial)*

> An auditor folds into the biggest hole in the world to file a routine survey,
> and finds a town having a *festival* in it. This was not in the brief.

- **Act:** 1 (arrival) · **The one feeling:** *"I get to play here."* — except
  Vesper would never admit it, so out loud it's *"I am WORKING here."*
- **Movement identity:** the **whole moveset is the toy** (GAME.md §4 — it's all
  available from the start). The Rim is the safe playground where chaining
  flash-jump → hop → grapple → teleport across the rooftops is *already fun before
  a single enemy* (Pillar #1). Taught by terrain, never by text.
- **Doubles as the hub:** respawn point, the always-on **sandbox testbed**
  (GAME.md §6/§7), Lumi's stage, the coffee shop, and the **trailhead portals**
  that light up as they unlock — the Rim *is* the overworld (no separate warp room).

## Story / quest beats — Q1 "First Survey"

1. **The fold-in.** Cold open in the dark between stars: Vesper unpins a generator
   like a pocket-watch, leans in to read its output, and the **survey** *is* the
   fall — the air-control tutorial. She lands hat-first in the Rim plaza. The
   fold-seam she came through stays **parked bright in the sky all game** (the
   skybox's north star, a quiet 3D-canon echo).
2. **Not in the brief.** She expected exhaust. She got bunting, a coffee stall,
   and a pop song. Her first act is to be **deeply professional about it** while
   **Star Motes** — loose pixels of starlight, the gentlest leak-creatures — drift
   through town bumping into lanterns. Bonking them back into the weave is the
   movement-and-"slice" tutorial. (Townsfolk think she's a very intense busker.)
3. **The idol.** **Lumi** is rehearsing on the stage; her vein-powered footlights
   keep guttering as the leak spikes. She clocks Vesper as another weird out-of-
   town cosplayer (she gets a lot of those) and is *delighted*. Vesper is not.
4. **Light the trail.** The leak is thickest down the **South Trail**, where the
   portal-sign sits dark. Reaching it across the town's rooftops (the movement
   gym) and stabilizing the local vein **lights the portal** — the first "I did a
   thing," and the gate out of the hub.
5. **First dusk.** The portal lights; the town's lanterns and Lumi's footlights
   catch from it; her evening set kicks in. Cozy payoff. Vesper, watching a town
   glow because of something she did, audibly fails to not care: *"…Tch. Fine.
   It's adequate lighting."* (The **day→dusk restage** is the cheap emotional
   lever the whole game reuses, per the 3D `rim.md`.)

## Layout — zone walkthrough

A cliffside town stacked up a canyon rim at golden hour (the mock board's hub),
the canyon always on your right shoulder. One screen-readable landmark per zone.

- **The Fold-In** (spawn). A soft upper ledge under the sky-seam, scattered with
  the town's clutter (crates, a stray festival drum) — the first thing you do is
  **knock stuff around**; the world reacts before a word of dialogue.
- **The Rim Plaza.** The carved **RIM** gate, petroglyph walls (deer, handprints,
  spirals — Sunwright echoes from 3D canon, here just atmosphere), the festival
  bunting. The town's "you are here." Minimap landmark.
- **The Coffee Shop.** The cliffside stall (laconic ranger shopkeep — see STORY
  §3 supporting). Sells coffee + trail supplies; the **noticeboard is the diegetic
  menu/help.** Coffee is the town's whole personality.
- **Lumi's Stage.** A little platform stage with **vein footlights** that flicker
  with the leak. Lumi rehearses here; ambient music source; the cosmetics/shop
  hook lives here later. The stage roof is a grapple-up landmark.
- **The Sandbox Nook.** A flat testbed terrace off the plaza — **prop-spawning
  always on here** (GAME.md §6) — where we (dev) and the player try new toys. The
  permanent playground. (Depends on M-physics for the full grab/float/constrain.)
- **The Rooftops** (the gym in a costume). Stepped roofs, lantern poles, awnings,
  a high bell-tower with a grapple node, and one **teleport gap** between two
  chimneys. The "addicting to just exist in" space — measured to the moveset's
  metrics so every verb has a place to sing.
- **The Trailheads.** Two portal-signs at the town's far arm: **South Trail**
  (lights in Q1 — the way out) and a second, still dark (a later area; teases the
  world map). Press **Up** to enter a lit one.

## Quests & collectibles

| # | Name | Kind | Design |
|---|------|------|--------|
| 1 | **First Survey** | main path | Clear the drifting Star Motes from the plaza/rooftops and reach the South Trail sign; stabilizing its vein lights the portal. Teaches the core moveset by routing you over the gym terrain. No fail. |
| 2 | **Roof Run** | movement | An optional rooftop circuit — flash-jump the awning gaps, grapple the bell-tower, teleport the chimney gap, land in the sandbox nook. Pure joy, zero stakes; the place playtesters will live. Stardust strung along the ideal line. |
| 3 | **Stardust in the Cracks** | exploration | Small stardust caches in alcoves and behind the festival stage — one tucked behind a **flutter-only** ledge (teaches the hover reach). |
| 4 | **The Survey Scope** | flavor/secret | A coin-op tourist binocular on the overlook rail (irresistible → teaches camera/zoom). Looking through it reveals the parked **sky-seam** up close + a far speck on the horizon (a Gemma tease). |

## Mobs

- **Star Mote** only — the gentlest leak-creature. Drifts, bumps, never threatens;
  the safe target dummy for the bonk/slice tutorial and the "creatures = leaking
  reality" introduction. Pops into stardust (the economy intro, GAME.md §8).

## Set-piece

The **dusk restage** itself (3D `rim.md`'s move): same town, golden-hour preset,
the portal-light cascading lantern-to-lantern as Lumi's set begins. The first
day→dusk swap demonstrates the restage lever — and costs a lighting preset.
Thereafter the Rim tracks story time (dusk after the Trail, *night* after the
Gulch's dread beat — her glow lights the empty plaza; cheapest emotional
instrument we own).

## Dialogue sketches

> **VESPER:** *(brushing juniper off her hat, surveying the bunting)* …This
> generator is producing *decorations.* That's not — output doesn't *do* that.
> **LUMI:** Ohh, nice costume! Are you the new busker? The horns girl said she
> was bringing a *rival.* You're way less yelling than I expected.
> **VESPER:** I am an **Auditor.** First Shift. I am *not* a—
> **LUMI:** Auditor! Cute. Stage left's open at eight.

> **SHOPKEEP:** Coffee?
> **VESPER:** I don't require sustenance, I'm a—
> **SHOPKEEP:** It's warm, it's bitter, you share it. 's about all we got figured
> out up here. *(slides her one anyway)*
> **VESPER:** *(does not drink it)* *(does not put it down either)*

> **VESPER:** *(the portal lights; the town glows)* …Tch. Fine. It's **adequate
> lighting.** Don't read into it. I have a *form* to file.

## Scope & reuse notes

- **Reuses:** the existing tilemap + one-way platforms (D024), the studio-authored
  Vesper sprite + clips (M10 exit proof), the moveset controller (M7), the
  inspector/editor for greyboxing, the day/dusk restage idea.
- **New (small):** a **portal prop** (lit/unlit trailhead sign + the press-Up
  enter — a tiny cartridge interactable), the Star Mote (one cheap sprite +
  drift+pop), the coffee-stall + stage props, festival clutter.
- **Leans on later milestones:** real **slicing**/death-pop is M12 (combat) — the
  Star Mote bonk uses the existing slice stub as placeholder; full **sandbox**
  grab/float is M-physics; the cosmetics shop is M13 (economy). All optional to
  the Q1 critical path (reach the portal).
- **Cut lines:** no shop interior; the shopkeep lives on the porch. The second
  trailhead stays dark until its area exists. If the rooftop gym reads as busy,
  thin the props — the *gaps* are the content, not the decor.
