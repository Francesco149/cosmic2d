## 1. Premise

You play **Vesper** — the white-haired mecha-girl architect who, in the 3D
`cosmic` game, becomes the cold **Auditor** that tries to erase the canyon.
cosmic2d is her **villain-origin**, and it gets to write her *before the ice set
in*: a proud, prickly, secretly-soft **tsundere** on her first solo assignment,
dropped into a cozy little canyon town she absolutely refuses to admit she likes.

The frame (from the 3D universe, §2 of its story bible): the universe is a
machine maintained by **cosmic architects** — a guild/utility called **the
Works** that counts time in **shifts** and thinks *life* is just exhaust. Vesper
is sent to **survey** one generator (Earth), space-folding into the **Grand
Canyon** — a seam in the machine's casing where the light leaks through as energy
veins and stardust. But the seam is **leaking too much**: raw reality is taking
**creature-form** (the mobs), and there's a warm little human town on the rim
that was *not* in her brief. Her job: stabilize the leaks, file a clean survey,
and get out before she gets attached. She fails the last part immediately.

In story terms, the three things cosmic2d is (GAME.md §1):
1. **a movement toy** — her reality-bending traversal (the architect's birthright);
2. **power-fantasy spectacle** — slicing the leak-hordes back into the weave;
3. **forgiving cozy exploration** — the town and canyon she comes to (*ugh*) care about.

## 2. The dread engine — how cozy and cosmic-dread coexist

The surface is warm: golden-hour rim town, coffee, an idol's stage, a clownish
rival. The dread is **dramatic irony**, applied with a light hand and escalating:

- **The player knows where she ends up.** Anyone who's met the 3D Auditor knows
  Vesper is destined to become the thing that *unrenders* worlds. Every cozy beat
  is shadowed by it.
- **Her "stabilization" is a rehearsal of the Sweep.** Filing a leak-creature
  back into the weave is the same gesture, smaller, that the Auditor will one day
  aim at *everything*. We never say it. The animation rhymes; the player feels it.
- **The Works is always upstream.** Terse work-orders arrive; the unspoken
  threat is that a generator flagged *unstable* gets escalated — and an Auditor is
  sent to clean it. Vesper stabilizing the leaks is, secretly, **buying the town
  time** (tsundere: "this has nothing to do with your stupid festival").

**Vesper's arc seed:** her tsundere warmth — the fluster, the helping-while-
denying-it — is *exactly what the Auditor buries.* cosmic2d shows the first crack
in the ice, played warm and funny, so the eventual freeze lands. Her future
line, *"Nothing I take is lost,"* is the thing she has **not yet learned to say.**

Keep it cozy up front (Act 1 = town + trail + the rival as pure comedy); let the
dread surface in the deep places (Whisper Gulch down) and in the work-orders.

## 3. Cast

Refs grounded in existing art; paths are Windows-side under `F:\Pictures\…`.

### Vesper — protagonist *(tsundere architect, the future Auditor)*

**Refs:** the white-haired mecha-girl (3D `cosmic` antagonist sheets; and the
player portrait in the cosmic2d mock board). **Look:** chibi ~2.5 heads,
white/silver hair, **cyan glowing eyes**, sleek white-and-gold mecha bodysuit
with crystalline **wing-plates**, icy-cool palette — softened for the cozy take
(rounder face, a real blush range, the cold edges not yet earned).

**Personality (cosmic2d take — we own this):** **tsundere.** Prideful, exacting,
treats her first solo survey like a performance review she *must* ace. Easily
flustered the instant the town's warmth gets to her; covers it with auditor-
speak and indignation. Under the airs: lonely, ferociously curious, and kind in
spite of herself. The **comedy** is the gap between her cold-auditor posturing
and her obviously soft heart; the **dread** is that, one day, the posturing wins.

**Voice:** clipped, formal, faintly metallic blips — but the cadence keeps
*cracking* into flustered stammers. (The 3D Auditor has "perfect cadence with
exactly one late stutter"; here the stutters are everywhere. The Auditor is who
learns to suppress them.)

**Gameplay role:** the player; the whole moveset (GAME.md §4) is hers. Her
architect nature justifies the reality-bending verbs and the energy economy; her
glow is a light source in the dark places.

> **VESPER:** Generator output… within tolerance. The *town* is outside my remit.
> *(a Star Mote bonks her hat)* — and you. Off. I'm **working.**

> **VESPER:** I am not *staying*. I am being *thorough*. There is a difference and
> it is on the form. *(re-pins her hat)* …Where's the festival.

### Gemma — recurring comedic antagonist *(the "Rival Architect")*

**Refs:** `F:\Pictures\oc\gemma-san\chibi` (turnaround + expression sheets).
**Look:** lavender hair, big dark curved **horns**, pink/purple **butterfly
wings**, **spade-tipped tail**, gold-trimmed sheer-black bodysuit, **padlock
earring**, pointed ears, fang-smug. Chibi. (Her wings rhyme with Vesper's
wing-plates and Mira's butterfly — a visual "architect" throughline.)

**The truth (the human's hook):** Gemma is **another cosmic architect** —
Vesper's peer and rival in the Works — who, on her own Earth posting, got
**hopelessly obsessed with human media** (anime, manga, the 2 a.m. dark-fantasy
TV the canyon's one motel got) and concluded that the *ideal form for communing
with humans* is a **dark-fantasy succubus villain.** The horns, wings, and tail
are **hard-light cosplay**; the chuunibyou persona is 100% self-inflicted. She
believes she is **blending in perfectly.** She is not.

**Personality:** theatrical, grandiose, **chuunibyou** to the marrow. Demands an
ever-escalating self-styled title; monologues through her own defeats;
"commands" leak-creatures she's certain are her devoted minions (they are just
wandering past). A spectacular, resilient **loser** — Team-Rocket trajectory:
arrives, declares dominion, gets bonked, vows return next area. Genuinely fond
of Vesper under all the villainy (and Vesper of her, tsundere-style — they're the
only two of their kind for a billion miles).

**The title gag:** she introduces herself with a different, longer chuuni title
every time; Vesper just says "Gemma."

**Voice:** lavish, sing-song, dramatic pauses and invented incantations
("*by the Ninefold Dark!*"); deflates into an ordinary, whiny architect the
instant she loses ("okay, OW, that's— that's a real attack, you can't just—").

**Gameplay role:** recurring comedic **boss**; she punctuates the main line, one
escalating duel per early area. **Not** a hub ally (human's call).

> **GEMMA:** Tremble! I am **Gemma, Duchess of the Abyssal Fold, Sovereign of
> Sweet Ruin** — and this canyon is now my **DOMAIN of eternal—**
> **VESPER:** You filed a *domain claim*? On a **leaking** generator? Do you have
> any idea what the stabilization paperwork on that looks like—
> **GEMMA:** It's called **AMBITION,** Vesper.

### Lumi — the local idol *(bunny-girl)*  **[proposed name]**

**Refs:** `F:\Pictures\oc\bunny-girl` (turnaround sheet + expressions +
`maple-screenshot.png` for the in-game vibe). **Look:** silver hair with
lavender tips, **rabbit-ear ornament**, halo + butterfly glow, frilly
purple-and-white idol stage outfit, red eyes, chibi ~2.3 heads. Expression range
includes a sharp **"cold smile."**

**The excuse (the human's):** she's a **human local idol** — the Rim town's
homegrown star — and the bunny-idol getup is just her **stage costume.** (The
cosmic resonance — a human dressing as a moon-rabbit while a literal cosmic being
looks on, baffled — is a free gag, not literal lore.)

**Personality:** sunny, generous **stage persona**; a sharper, tired, business-
savvy self **off-stage** (the "cold smile" — she's the one actually holding the
town's morale together while the veins flicker). She and Vesper click as **two
performers who can't drop the act** — Lumi sees right through Vesper's auditor
armor and needles her about it warmly.

**Voice:** bright, melodic, idol-patter blips on stage; flatter and dryer off it.

**Gameplay role:** **hub idol** — a little stage in the Rim town (flavor +
ambient music + a small cosmetics/shop hook) — plus the **first side-quest** (her
show's leak-powered centerpiece gets dragged off down the trail; retrieve it).

> **LUMI** *(on stage):* Thank you, Riiim! You're glowing tonight — almost as
> bright as **me!** ☆
> **LUMI** *(off stage, flat):* The stage lights run off the veins. They gutter
> mid-set and I'm finished. …No pressure, surveyor.

## 4. The cosmic creatures (mobs)

**Diegetically:** where the seam wears thin, the machine's raw stuff **takes
creature-form** — cute, eerie-pretty, harmless-looking until there's a horde.
Slicing one **files it back into the weave** (the proto-Sweep gesture — the
dread, §2). They drop **stardust/currency** (GAME.md §8). Roster lifted from the
mock board (great cute-readable silhouettes that "pop satisfyingly", GAME.md §5):

|-----|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Star Mote** | a loose pixel of starlight | drifts, bumps, harmless trash | Rim / everywhere |
| **Fold Slime** | folded-space blob | hops, **splits** when sliced | South Trail |
| **Pebble Drone** | a rock that learned to roll | charges in a straight line | South Trail |
| **Cactus Satellite** | desert flora + orbiting spikes | stationary hazard, orbiting spines | South Trail |
| **Glyph Beetle** | petroglyph-shelled crawler | armored; crack the shell first | Whisper Gulch |

| **Mirror Wisp** | a reflection that came loose | blinks/teleports, mirrors your moves | later |
| **Tiny Floodfish** | coolant-channel fish | swarms in water | later (flood area) |

## 5. World & areas

cosmic2d **reuses the Grand Canyon seam-world** of the 3D game (mocks confirm:
Rim, Whisper Gulch, Echo Reach, Sun Vault, Night Cave, Raven Exchange are all
shared locations). The **Rim** is the cozy town hub and respawn point (GAME.md
§6/§8). The mock world board has the full first-arc set; cosmic2d builds out from
the Rim:

1. **Rim Hub** — cozy rim town: hub, respawn, the sandbox testbed, Lumi's stage,
   the coffee shop, and the trailhead **portals** (press Up to enter).
2. **South Trail** — first region: open sunlit canyon ledges, the horde-combat
   and one-way-platform intro, and **Gemma's debut** at the overlook.
3. **Whisper Gulch** — vertical slot canyon: the grapple/teleport gym and the
   **first real cosmic-dread beat** down in the dark.

*Later (mock board, not yet designed):* Echo Falls, Echo Reach (Dwellings), Sun
Vault, Night Cave / The Hush, Blue Garden Spring, Fold Observatory, Raven
Exchange, and the **Bright Inside / Final Fold**. Per-map pages in
[`docs/maps/`](maps/README.md).

## 6. The opening quest spine

The **main line** is *Vesper's survey* — stabilize the leaks, area by area.
**Gemma** interrupts it; **Lumi's** is the first side-quest. (Drops/economy per
GAME.md §8; combat per §5 — combat itself is the M12 milestone, so these are
design targets that lean on the moveset we have today.)

| # | Quest | Map | Kind | Beat |
|---|-------|-----|------|------|
| Q1 | **First Survey** | Rim Hub | main · tutorial | Unfold in; the town's rattled by leak-critters; learn the moveset by terrain while clearing them; light the South Trail portal. Meet Lumi + the shop. |
| Q2 | **The Show Must Go On** | South Trail | side (early) | Lumi's stage-gem (her show's leak-powered centerpiece) got dragged down-trail by a Fold Slime. First real horde + traversal; bring it back before the evening set. |
| Q3 | **Domain of the Dread Duchess** | South Trail (overlook) | main · boss | Gemma debuts, declares the canyon her domain; comedic duel (Thread Imp adds + hard-light theatrics). She loses, flees down the gulch vowing return. |
| Q4 | **The Whisper Below** *(seed)* | Whisper Gulch | main | Follow her down; grapple/teleport descent; the deepest leak yet — the first true cosmic-dread beat, and the Works' first ominous work-order. |
